Is the Coolizi portable cooler legit, or is it basically just a fan?
Short answer: yes, it's a real product that genuinely cools the air right around you, but only if you go in with the right expectations.

Coolizi belongs to a category called personal evaporative coolers. There's no compressor and no installation. You fill a small tank with water (a few ice cubes help), plug it in, and a fan pulls air through a damp wick. As the water evaporates it draws heat out of that airstream, so what blows out feels clearly cooler than what a normal desk fan gives you. That part is real physics, not marketing fluff.

The catch, and this is where a lot of the angry one-star reviews come from, is the word personal. It's built to cool the pocket of air directly in front of it: your desk, your side of the sofa, your pillow at night. It will not turn a hot living room into a cold one, and anyone selling it as a "mini air conditioner" that replaces a proper AC is overselling it. Sit within arm's reach and it works. Stand a few metres away and you'll barely feel it.

Two honest caveats before you buy. First, evaporative coolers add moisture to the air, so they perform best in dry heat and underwhelm in already-humid weather. If your summers are muggy rather than baking, temper your expectations. Second, you have to refill the water, and the cooling is strongest right after a fresh, cold fill, then fades as the water warms up.

On the legitimacy question itself: it ships, it's returnable, and it does exactly what an evaporative cooler is supposed to do. It's the same basic hardware sold under several brand names, so you're not paying for some patented breakthrough. You're paying for a quiet, low-wattage unit that's cheap to run and easy to carry from room to room. The single units are priced reasonably; the multi-packs are where it gets expensive, so only buy as many as you'll actually use day to day.

So is it a scam? No. Is it a magic whole-room AC for the price of a fan? Also no. It's a genuinely useful spot-cooler for one person in a warm room, and judged on that it earns its keep.
